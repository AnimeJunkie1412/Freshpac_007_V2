# Current system screenshot review

The screenshots show a classic desktop operations system: compact, fast for trained users, but visually dense and toolbar-heavy.

## Patterns to preserve

- Fast module access: Sales, Invoiced, Customers, Stock, Equipment, Reports and Notes.
- Global search at the top of major modules.
- Dense tables for office workflows such as call list, stock list and sales invoices.
- Customer account tabs for setup, prices, equipment, rental, sales history and notes.
- Stock records with internal product code, description, product group, pack size, list price, tax code and notes.
- Call list filters for contact day, delivery day and van.
- Customer history context: past orders, price lists and shopping lists.

## Problems to improve

- Toolbars rely on small icons that are hard to understand without training.
- Important warnings are not visually strong enough.
- Large forms feel flat and tiring to scan.
- Tabs are useful, but need clearer grouping and better spacing.
- Tables need better filtering, sticky headers, status chips and quick actions.
- Notes and special instructions need prominent placement because they drive real-world decisions.

## New design direction

Professional and semi-compact:

- Keep dense tables for staff, but use cleaner row spacing and visible filters.
- Use cards for dashboard counters, warnings and action queues.
- Use tabs for customer records, but give each tab a focused layout.
- Use Freshpac orange sparingly for primary actions, highlights and active states.
- Use charcoal for navigation and headings.
- Use warm backgrounds and white panels to avoid a cold enterprise feel.
- Make destructive actions deliberate and confirmation-led.
